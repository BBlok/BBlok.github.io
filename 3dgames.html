3D Game Projects

When it comes to designing a game, designing levels for both 2D and 3D games is one of the things that I enjoy doing the most, including the architecture and aesthetics of the levels, placement of objects, lighting, and finally testing how these components synthesize the gaming experience for the player. This page contains some of the works that I have done in designing 3D levels for a first-person shooter experience.

Unreal Tournament 4 CTF Map: Egocentric Empanadas

Egocentric Empanadas is a Capture the Flag (CTF) multiplayer map developed for Unreal Tournament 4 where two opposing teams fight against each other to capture the other team’s flag to return it to their base to score points. This is a map designed for 8 players (4 players per team) with a focus on fast-pace gameplay.

This is an open space arena floating in space with almost no enclosed rooms. Its overall shape is lineal so that the bases are at opposite ends of the map and are directly facing each other.This map also makes extensive use of jump pads with long trajectories for players to quickly fly around the map while dodging enemies and engaging in mid-air combat.

Mods - Classic Doom Singleplayer Level

One of my first experiences in designing levels started with making levels for Doom back in mid 2000s. When I started learning to use the doom builder level editor and started making my own levels, it became one of my favorite hobbies to do at the time, and as I kept progressing in my level design skills and making more maps, I started to learn and get some experience in various aspects about level desing, including making interesting and engaging level architecture and aesthetics, lighting, a little bit of cinematography, object/enemy positioning, and trying out several desings to see how these elements come into play and affect the game flow once they are combined.

Mods - Killing Floor 2

In this project, I decided to challenge myself to make a level for Killing Floor 2 from scratch using the Killing Floor 2 SDK (which is powered by Unreal Engine). Being my first level made using this SDK and my first project working with static meshes and BSP brushes, I decided to start with a relatively simple project and make a level based on the Temple multiplayer level from Goldeneye 007 to apply some of the techniques that I have learned so far in Unreal Engine such as UV map/unwrap, material creation, diffuse and normal maps, and level contruction using a modular set of 3d models. To make this level, I created a series of modular pieces (static meshes) in 3ds Max and later used them to compose it in a modular way. In order to match the mood of the game , I also decided to give the level a dark/sinister feel by manipulating the lighting in the environment.
